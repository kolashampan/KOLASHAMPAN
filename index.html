Mathematical Definition: Let \(f: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) be a continuous function.

Proof: For any \(x \in \mathbb{R}\), let \(h\) be an infinitesimally small positive quantity. The limit of the difference quotient as \(h\) approaches zero is defined as follows: \[f'(x) = \lim_{{h \to 0}} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}\]
